- `P0`: blocks MVP functional completeness or must be closed before claiming staging-entry readiness for that business loop.

## Executive Summary

The current local happy path is not equivalent to MVP functional completeness.

The strongest local evidence today is:

- student login/session, profile logout, existing seeded authorization, `practice`, and `mock_exam` happy paths;
- system ops and content ops list/filter/entry validation;
- selected local `redeem_code` generation source and student redemption messaging;
- route and service scaffolding for many required areas;
- mock-provider-first AI/RAG service tests and redaction-oriented evidence.

The largest MVP gaps are still write-loop and propagation gaps:

- content ops cannot yet complete full question/material/paper authoring, composition, publish, and student consumption as one proven loop;
- AI explanation/hint/scoring exists in service layers but is still visibly unavailable or incomplete in key student UI flows;
- model_config primary/fallback selection is not proven as an end-to-end business runtime configuration loop;
- system ops user/org_auth/redeem_code management is partially local and still lacks requirement-level granularity.

`stagingDecision`: `do_not_advance_to_staging_until_p0_mvp_gap_tasks_are_closed_or_product-approved-as-deferred`

## Why This Was Missed

The earlier process allowed `local happy path`, `MVP acceptance`, and `staging entry` to blur together. A route, fixture-backed page, or service unit test sometimes became treated as functional completion even when the requirement demanded a role-to-role business loop.

The process fix is to make every future task carry an AC-to-runtime matrix:

- requirement AC;
- implemented route/service/UI;
- local seed or fixture dependency;
- browser/API evidence;
- downstream student/business effect;
- audit/ai_call_log evidence where required;
- explicit deferred decision with owner approval if not implemented.

No future closeout should claim MVP completeness from route existence, scaffold presence, fixture-only UI, or read-only list validation.
